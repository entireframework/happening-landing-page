A European Programme for Creative Entrepreneurs

A one-year European programme resulting in a co-created set of open-access digital tools and a network that can be used, shared and adapted by young creatives across Europe.

WHAT IS HAPPENING

HAPPENING is built on a simple idea: entrepreneurship is about making an idea happen.

We support emerging creative entrepreneurs in:


  • Developing their own projects
  • Understanding their ecosystems (local and European)
  • Strengthening their skills and confidence
  • Evaluating their impact as cultural entrepreneurs
  • Shaping their own definition of cultural entrepreneurship


HAPPENING is supported by the Erasmus+ Programme of the European Union, led by a consortium of 4 organisations:

ALTER EGO (X) (A civil association advocating for independence of the cultural sector)   x   Energie alter-native (A cultural association supporting performing arts through festivals and artist development)   x   Entire Framework (A technology consultancy designing digital solutions)   x   ICHEC (A Brussels-based business school fostering sustainable innovation in Cultural and Creative Industries).

WHO WE'RE LOOKING FOR

We're seeking 27 young and emerging creatives:

Ready to develop their projects, skills and autonomy within a European collaborative framework, and eager to shape European cultural future.


Fields

  • Performing arts (theatre, music, dance, contemporary circus, etc.)
  • Cinematography
  • Creative media (podcasts, audiovisual tools, sensitive and author-driven formats)
  • Visual arts
  • Cultural managers, producers and cultural workers in these fields

Requirements

  • Resident in Belgium, France or Italy (no nationality requirement)
  • Aged 20 to 35
  • Comfortable working in English
  • Motivated to engage in collective, reflective and experimental processes
  • Open to working in an international and diverse environment
  • Agrees with our core values

WHAT TO EXPECT

A ten-month European Programme (October 2026 – May 2027), involving:

  • Training sessions and collaborative workshops along the year with partner organisations, to help identify the needs and challenges of projects in the cultural entrepreneurship field
  • The co-creation of a set of open-access digital tools and a vision for the future network that will be used, shared and adapted by young and aspiring creatives across Europe
  • Networking with participants and partner organisations
  • Delivering a Youthpass, a European certification of non-formal competences developed


Participants from diverse artistic disciplines and professional profiles will co-create a heterogeneous ecosystem of tools through real exchanges of practices — ensuring that future resources are adaptable to a wide range of creative entrepreneurs in the future.


Your journey includes an online kick-off to get started, three hands-on modules exploring ecosystems, entrepreneurial skills, and ethical practices, a trip to Brussels to connect with the wider community, collaborative sessions to build our shared platform, and a chance to reflect on everything you've experienced.
